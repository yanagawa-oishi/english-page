Spreading to the world
Global Academy Design

Yanagawa High School had always been connected to the world.
Now, let's go beyond borders!
The world is moving towards the SDGs, a common goal.By 2025, Yanagawa High School hopes to have offices in all ASEAN nations (South East Asia) and to become a global campus, with international students accounting for one-third of our student population. In 2016, Yanagawa Global Academy opened its first overseas affiliated junior high school in Nakhon Si Thammarat, Thailand. And since last year, its graduates from Thailand have been studying at Yanagawa Senior High School. Our school has offices in nine countries, including seven in Asia, Australia and the UK, and our aim is to have international students in all grades and departments in the future. From the world to Yanagawa, and from Yanagawa to the world. We will continue to pursue education for the next generation.

Acceptance of International Students

There are currently about 80 international students enrolled in Yanagawa High School. We have five different Japanese language courses at different levels. International students are working hard to achieve N1, the most difficult level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test. In addition, first-year students (international students) study in the JSL* course for one semester, and we have a system in place that allows students to concentrate on studying Japanese for four months from the start of school in April until the summer vacation.
If you are studying for a year, you can request to continue your JSL course after the 4-month period or to take classes in Japanese with Japanese students.
We also offer a short course such as the Summer program. *JSL:Japanese as a second language.JSL is offered only in the International Department.

International Student Support Center

We established an International Student Support Center in the school in response to a rise in the number of international students.The center provides a wide range of support to international students, including Japanese language training, residency requirement, career and lifestyle counseling, and organizing events such as tea ceremonies.
